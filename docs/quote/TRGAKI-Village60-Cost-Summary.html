01 — The Application

One platform for the whole operation

Village60 is a single, secure web platform built specifically for retirement home operations — replacing the spreadsheets, paper files, and separate tools that most homes end up juggling.

Resident records, clinical care, medication rounds, inventory and purchasing, monthly billing, payment tracking, occupancy reports, and enquiries from prospective families all live in one place. Administrators can see across the whole organisation; care staff only see the homes they work in.

The system works on desktop, tablet, and mobile, and is available in English, Sinhala, and Tamil. Each staff member can choose whichever language they are most comfortable with.

Built-in across the platform

Security is built in from the start: staff sign in with encrypted sessions and strong passwords, and the system locks accounts after repeated failed attempts. Admin and Care roles control who can see what, scoped down to individual homes. Sign-in events, medication records, inventory movements, and billing entries are all logged with a clear audit trail.

Billing supports multiple currencies per home, with accurate accounting down to the smallest unit. Monthly resident charges are generated automatically on the first of each month. Families can access privacy-safe resident profile pages via QR codes, and the database is backed up daily with a documented restore process. Every list and directory in the application has search, filtering, and pagination built in, so staff can find what they need quickly.

02 — Scope of Delivery

Implemented features & services

i.

Platform Foundation

The foundation covers everything needed to run the system in production: secure sign-in, a simple two-tier role model, and an interface available in English, Sinhala, and Tamil. The application works well on any screen size, and we handle deployment, daily backups, and the operational runbook from day one.

AuthenticationRoles & permissions3 languagesBackups
ii.

Homes, Wards & Residents

You can manage multiple homes from one account, each with its own wards, bed capacity, and monthly rates. Resident records cover the full lifecycle — intake (with duplicate checking), photos, next of kin, power of attorney, nurse assignment, and departures. Each resident can also have a shareable public profile with a QR code for families.

Multi-siteBed capacityResident recordsQR profiles
iii.

Clinical Care & Medication

Care staff can manage scheduled and as-needed (PRN) medications for each resident, along with conditions and allergies. The daily Medication Administration Record (MAR) shows what was given, when, and by whom — and stock levels update automatically as doses are recorded.

Daily MARPRN supportAllergies & conditionsAuto stock deduction
iv.

Inventory & Procurement

Each home keeps its own item catalog with live stock levels and a full history of every movement. There is a supplier directory and a complete purchase order workflow — from draft through approval, sending, receiving, and closure. When medication stock runs low, the system suggests reorders.

Stock ledgerPurchase ordersSuppliersReorder alerts
v.

Billing & Finance

Monthly resident charges are generated automatically, and invoices can be raised for both resident and home operating accounts. Payments are recorded against multiple methods, with oldest charges settled first. Registration fees, deposits, medication costs, and procurement all feed into a single ledger so nothing falls through the cracks.

vi.

Analytics & Growth

Dashboards cover occupancy and census trends, monthly cash flow, expense breakdowns, admissions, stay duration, demographics, and staff workload. A public enquiry form feeds a waiting-list pipeline, with metrics on conversion rates and available capacity.

vii.

Administration & Daily Operations

Administrators can manage staff with per-home assignments and floaters, configure organisation settings, and track tasks with priorities and due dates. The main dashboard highlights overdue payments, tasks that need attention, and upcoming resident birthdays — the things that matter most on a busy morning.
